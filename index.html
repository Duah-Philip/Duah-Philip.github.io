Biography

I am a Master’s Student in Mining and Minerals Engineering at TU Bergakademie Freiberg. Prior to this I worked with Kinross Gold Corporation within their technical services. Over the course of my master’s degree, I have worked as a mining engineer and Business Development intern with General Atomics Europe GmbHand also within the area of computational minerals engineering and analytics at the Helmholtz Institute Freiberg.

Currently, I am a visiting research scholar at [Missouri University of Science and technology] (https://www.mst.edu/)in the United States. I am interested in the application of technological methodologies to disentangle the complex interaction between mineral resource extraction, sustainability and Business to advance our scientific understanding and forward these insights into optimizing industrial processes. To accomplish these goals, I draw upon computer science, mathematics, and mining engineering. I look forward to transferring the skills and expertise I will gain from these into the real world.

Besides research activities, I am active with Amnesty international, Progressive international, and other initiatives in Germany (Sachsen) aimed at positively impacting communities as a whole.

Prediction of Rock Fragmentation using a Novel Hybrid Evolutionary Algorithm and Extreme Gradient Boosting Machine

Rock fragmentation by blasting is regarded as one of the most substantial activities of the mine production cycle owing to its proportionate effect (cost) on subsequent operations such as loading, hauling and crushing. Being able to predict the fragmentation of a rock mass by blasting offers substantial advantage in providing muck pile with a desirable size distribution for crushing.
