Biography

I am a Master’s Student in Mining and Minerals Engineering at TU Bergakademie Freiberg. Prior to this I worked with Kinross Gold Corporation within their technical services. Over the course of my master’s degree, I have worked as a Mining Engineer and Business Development intern with General Atomics Europe GmbH and also within the area of computational minerals engineering and analytics at the Helmholtz Institute Freiberg.

I was also a visiting research scholar at Missouri University of Science and technology in the United States. I am interested in the application of technological methodologies to disentangle the complex interaction between mineral resource extraction, sustainability and Business, to advance our scientific understanding and forward these insights into optimizing industrial processes. To accomplish these goals, I draw upon computer science, mathematics, and mining engineering. I look forward to transferring the skills and expertise I will gain from these into the real-world.

Besides research activities,I describe myself as a rabid “POLITIVIST”- part political, part activist. I believe that it isn’t too late to create a better world- One free of Human Rights abuse, Racism, or Fascism. In cognizance of these themes, I am actively involved with several organizations whose missions align with the aforementioned ones. One such organization is Amnesty international, Germany. Through them, I founded the local branch in Freiberg, Saxony.

On a number of occasions, I have participated in panel discussions on Human Rights and Feminist movements. I am also involved with Saxony schools to help shape diversity and internationalization. In recent times, I have been with Progressive international, an organization which aims to unite, organize, and mobilize progressive forces worldwide for positive change, and other initiatives in Germany aimed at positively impacting communities as a whole. I talk about Politics a lot, and in particular global Foreign Policy.

Since World War II, there has been a growing tendency to view foreign policy as a domestic policy tool. A more realistic, pragmatic, and proactive foreign policy must emerge. I label this as the “Total Foreign Policy System” (TFPS). The first principle of such a functioning system is secure and cumulative access to resources pursuant to the national interest. It is clear that foreign policy mistakes have been made, their effects being felt in recent years, but what are the most critical errors that need to be rectified? And what shall be the economic ramifications on domestic policies when such Total Foreign Policy Systems are implemented? Does Germany need such a “Total Foreign Policy System”? Does America need such a “Total Foreign Policy System”? Start from here and here
