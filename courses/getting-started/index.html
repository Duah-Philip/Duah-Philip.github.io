Exchange With The Rector of TU Bergakademie Freiberg

I honored the invitation of the Rector of TU Bergakademie Freiberg, Prof. Dr. Klaus-Dieter Barbknecht who is also the current chairman of the State Rectors' Conference of Saxony, Germany.

Duah Philip
Aug 29, 2019
Image credit: TU Bergakademie Freiberg

On August 28th, an official meeting was held with Prof. Dr. Klaus-Dieter Barbknecht, the Rector of TU Bergakademie Freiberg who also serves as chairman of the State Rectors' Conference of Saxony, Germany. Participants included the director of the University Graduate and Research Academy, Dr. Kristina Wopat, as well as student council members (StuRa), the Feminism Group, and the International Center’s working group for foreign students (AKAS).

In my brief presentation, I detailed my ambitions for Freiberg and the University. As the Founder, Spokesperson, and President of Amnesty International Freiberg, a human rights group comprising over forty students from 13 different countries studying and living in Freiberg, I mentioned that the university authorities must show their full solidarity at all times for minority students.

The rise of hate groups and right-wing extremists in Freiberg and the state (Saxony) in general warrants a safe space for people with migration backgrounds, refugees, Black people, and other minorities studying in the University. Taking a pragmatic approach to dealing with societal acceptance of minority students within the city will not be easy; however, in order to overcome societal challenges, collaboration, solidarity, and common ideas will be necessary.

In his remarks, the rector expressed his strong commitment to ensuring the well-being of international students. He stressed how important it is to him that students in Freiberg live in safety. A number of his encouraging pieces of advice focused on what to do if a student falls victim to abuse or attack within the city. We concluded the meeting by discussing the possibility of holding a student parliament. The purpose of this special event is to engage Germans and international students at the TU Bergakademie Freiberg in human rights discussions while fostering civil society engagement.
